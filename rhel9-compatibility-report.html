RHEL 8 → RHEL 9 Compatibility Assessment

ICCommand Application — Eastern Michigan University

The IT department is upgrading the production server from RHEL 8 to RHEL 9. This report assesses the ICCommand codebase against three known risk areas: OpenSSL 3.0 migration, binary dependency rebuilds, and PCRE2 engine changes.

1. OpenSSL 3.0 (The "Crypto" Break) Action Required

LDAP Connection High Risk

The LDAP configuration in config/services.yaml:33-40 connects to ad.emich.edu on port 389 with encryption: none. This is the biggest compatibility concern:

  • RHEL 9's stricter crypto policy may reject the unencrypted LDAP bind. The underlying OpenLDAP client library is upgraded from 2.4.x to 2.6.x on RHEL 9 with stricter defaults.
  • Passwords are transmitted in cleartext during LDAP authentication (used in staging and production per config/packages/security.yaml:96-100, 114-118).
Recommended Fix — Upgrade to LDAPS or STARTTLS: 
# Option A: LDAPS (preferred - encrypted from the start)
Symfony\Component\Ldap\Adapter\ExtLdap\Adapter:
  arguments:
    - host: ad.emich.edu
      port: 636
      encryption: ssl
      options:
        protocol_version: 3
        referrals: false

# Option B: STARTTLS (upgrades plaintext connection to TLS)
Symfony\Component\Ldap\Adapter\ExtLdap\Adapter:
  arguments:
    - host: ad.emich.edu
      port: 389
      encryption: tls
      options:
        protocol_version: 3
        referrals: false
Before changing: Confirm with your AD/LDAP team whether ad.emich.edu supports LDAPS on port 636 or STARTTLS on port 389.
